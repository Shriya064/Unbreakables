Vinesh Phogat

Regarded as one of the finest wrestlers to arise from india, vinesh phogat has a strong wrestling heritage behind her. Inspired by wrestler father, she started to practise after being introduced to the sport at an early age. Her very struggle began with facing social barriers and setbacks. Along with her cousins, she was neither supported nor allowed to take part in the wrestling matches. At the age of just nine, she had to cope with the untimely death of her father. She came under conversation after making an international mark by winning bronze at the 2013 asian wrestling championship at the age of 19. She carried on her fine form at the 2015 asian championships in doha, where she clinched another silver medal. While her career seemed to be on a steep rise at this point, she suffered a huge setback in the following year at the 2016 paralympic games. Despite her knee injury she continued to work for her dream. She announced a strong comeback into wrestling, as she clinched a silver medal at the asian wrestling championship. Vinesh phogat was a medal favorite but nothing went right for the star indian wrestler at tokyo 2020. She lost the quarterfinal by fall to vanesa kaladzinskaya of belarus. Unfair criticism of her performance had led to her feeling distressed, leaving her wondering whether she should even return to the mat. But she was determined to compete once again. Vinesh went on to win many medals including recent gold at cwg 2022 in birmingham to become an outstanding wrestler! In word of confucius, "our greatest glory is not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall."

He started playing chess professionally at age 8, conclusively won the tournament with a decent score, and became the youngest u. S. Champion when he was just 14. His gains commonly resulted from surprise attacks or counterattacks rather than from the collection of small upper hands, yet his play remained positionally sound. Soon after the candidates tournament in 1959, he dropped out of high school to reserve more time for chess. In 1970, he made his comeback to chess, and after closing with a seven-game winning streak, won the interzonal tournament. His significant career path went through the cold war period and glittered with his exceptional struggle. In 1972, he indirectly worked as an unlikely weapon for the u. S. Against soviet russia. The game of chess between spassky and fischer had come to represent the cold war itself. His passion for puzzles was combined with endless hours of studying and playing chess. His incredible comeback comes exactly 20 years after he won the championship and he played his first pawn. The atmosphere of this whole championship was heavy and transient. With such uniqueness in his game, he ended up on a show. His quiet, straightforward words influenced many generations and it's "chess demands total concentration."

Erica Abidal

He never limited himself to one country, but rather he challenged himself across three different countries and succeeded in each. Suddenly, his journey is cut short by the detection of a liver tumor, necessitating a transplant.

"it was complicated to come back, but like i always say, sport enabled me to have this mental strength to fight. " - eric abidal.

Some sporting comebacks are exceptionally motivating and magnificent. This is one of them! ! It has been glorious and unbreakable in sporting parameters since he began his career. Pullela Gopichand

If one says that pullela gopichand is the man behind the success of indian badminton, then it won't be an exaggeration, rather it's reality!! Former olympian pullela gopichand is the coach who helped both Saina Nehwal and PV Sindhu to their Olympic medals. Currently, he is the chief national coach for the indian national badminton team. He dominated badminton in india after the 1990s, securing five consecutive national singles titles between 1996 and 2000. In 1994, while playing a mens doubles rubber in the team championship, he collided with his partner and got injured. His acl was torn and he went through three surgeries in a couple of years. Not many badminton players have managed to reach the top after suffering the kind of injury he went through in 1994, which makes his career all the more magnificent. Going into the 2001 all england open, gopichand was neither considered a favourite nor a long shot. The summit clash was a relatively one-sided game with the dominance of india. Gopichand was only 27 when he clinched the coveted title. Every indian badminton supporter is now aware that pullela gopichand is one of only two players to win the mens singles crown at the eminent all-england championships. In Pullela's words, "the only answer to negativity is a new, improved positive version of your work."

Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali started practicing boxing when someone stole his bike when he was 12, and he wanted to fight the thief. Ali soon began more rigorous training, hitting heavy punching bags to strengthen his swing and practicing in front of a mirror to improve his reflexes. When floyd patterson defended himself for the first time in 1965, his journey began with exceptional magnificence. In march 1966, ali refused to be worked into the armed forces, and he has been exiled from boxing for his firm stance against the vietnam war. Ali was granted a license to box in 1970. He was a giant of his time, a cogent and loud fighter whose influence was felt far beyond the ring. He never limited his journey to sports itself rather, he made an impression on many issues. After too much struggle his fast fists and indomitable personality outmatched sports and captivated the world. He was more than the sum of his athletic gifts. Swift thinking, a buoyant personality, brash self-confidence, and an evolving set of personal convictions encouraged a magnetism that the ring alone could not contain. Ali, in one of his inspiring speeches said, "it isn't the mountains ahead that wear you down. It's the pebble in your shoe. " legend for a reason!!
